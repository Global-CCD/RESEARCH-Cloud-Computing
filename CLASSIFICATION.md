The classification of web hosting and cloud computing involves multiple layers, ranging from broad financial industry standards to granular functional service models.

### 1. Macro Industry Taxonomy
This level follows global standards used for economic reporting and stock market classification.

*   **NAICS (North American Industry Classification System):**
    *   **518210:** *Computing Infrastructure Providers, Data Processing, Web Hosting, and Related Services.* This is the "catch-all" category for cloud providers, data centers, and hosting firms.
*   **GICS (Global Industry Classification Standard):**
    *   **Sector:** Information Technology (45)
    *   **Industry Group:** Software & Services (4510)
    *   **Industry:** IT Services (451020)
    *   **Sub-Industry:** Internet Services & Infrastructure (45102030) — Includes companies providing data centers, cloud networking, and web hosting.

---

### 2. Functional Service Hierarchy (The "XaaS" Stack)
This hierarchy organizes companies based on the **level of the technology stack** they provide and manage.

| Level | Category | Examples of Companies/Services |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Layer 4** | **SaaS (Software as a Service)** | Salesforce, Microsoft 365, Slack, Shopify |
| **Layer 3** | **PaaS (Platform as a Service)** | Heroku, Google App Engine, Azure App Service |
| **Layer 2** | **IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)** | AWS (EC2/S3), DigitalOcean, Linode, Vultr |
| **Layer 1** | **Colocation / Bare Metal** | Equinix, Digital Realty, Iron Mountain |

**Emerging Functional Niches:**
*   **CaaS (Containers as a Service):** Kubernetes-managed environments (e.g., GKE, Amazon EKS).
*   **FaaS (Function as a Service):** "Serverless" computing (e.g., AWS Lambda, Vercel).
*   **BaaS (Backend as a Service):** Database and auth services for mobile/web (e.g., Firebase, Supabase).
*   **DRaaS (Disaster Recovery as a Service):** Specialized cloud backup and failover.

---

### 3. Web Hosting Specific Taxonomy
While "Cloud" focuses on scalable resources, "Web Hosting" usually refers to pre-packaged environments for websites.

#### **A. Infrastructure Deployment Models**
1.  **Shared Hosting:** Thousands of websites share a single server's resources. (Entry-level: Bluehost, HostGator).
2.  **VPS (Virtual Private Server):** A physical server partitioned into "virtual" private environments. (Mid-level: SiteGround, A2 Hosting).
3.  **Dedicated Hosting:** A physical server leased entirely to one client.
4.  **Cloud Hosting:** Resources spread across multiple interconnected servers for high availability.

#### **B. Application-Specific (Specialized) Hosting**
*   **Managed WordPress Hosting:** Optimized specifically for WP performance and security (e.g., WP Engine, Kinsta).
*   **E-commerce Hosting:** Optimized for platforms like Magento or WooCommerce, often with PCI-compliance.
*   **Gaming Hosting:** Low-latency servers for multiplayer games like Minecraft or Counter-Strike.
*   **Reseller Hosting:** Allows agencies to buy bulk resources and brand them as their own hosting service.

---

### 4. Operational Taxonomy (Managed vs. Unmanaged)
Companies are often categorized by the amount of **support** they provide:

*   **Unmanaged (Self-Managed):** The provider gives you the "keys" to the server (OS, IP). You are responsible for security, updates, and optimization. (Typical for IaaS like AWS or DigitalOcean).
*   **Managed Services:** The provider handles server maintenance, security patches, backups, and monitoring. (Typical for premium web hosts and Managed Service Providers - MSPs).
*   **Professional Services:** Project-based consulting for cloud migration, architecture design, or cybersecurity auditing.

---

### 5. Physical Infrastructure Classification (The Data Center)
If a company owns the building, they are classified by their **Tier Rating (Uptime Institute)**:
*   **Tier I & II:** Basic capacity, limited or no redundancy.
*   **Tier III:** Concurrently maintainable (no shutdown needed for maintenance).
*   **Tier IV:** Fault-tolerant (multiple isolated systems; highest reliability).
